## 1. Scope

This contract defines primary/backup, leader election, and fault takeover boundaries for industrial-grade multi-coordinator deployment.

Related Documents:

- `execution_plane_contract.md`

## 2. Goals

- Avoid coordinator becoming a single point.
- Ensure only one active leader at any moment is responsible for critical control actions.
- Make leader switch not break lease, dispatch, and recovery truth.

## 3. Key Objects

- `CoordinatorNode`
- `LeaderLease`
- `LeadershipEpoch`
- `FailoverDecision`

## 4. Rules

- Leader identity must be generated by authoritative backend; must not rely on local memory.
- Any leader switch must increment `leadership_epoch`.
- Follower must not execute actions requiring leader authority, such as global repair, queue reconciliation, global freeze.
- Old leader after losing epoch must not continue writing control plane results.

## 5. Election Requirements

- Election mechanism must support at minimum: acquire, renew, expire, reject old writes after preemption.
- Leader lease should be shorter than on-call recovery window, but not short enough to cause frequent jitter.

## 6. Closure Conclusion

The essence of HA coordinator is not "opening a few more nodes", but clearly defining control authority, epoch, and stale leader protection.
